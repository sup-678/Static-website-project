Attraction Spots in Náchod

The Renaissance  Náchod chateau  really stands on a hill and in the interiors you can see valuable collections of paintings, orders, medals and weapons. A nice view of the city offers a terrace with a gazebo , you can also see the tower with the famine and the medieval cellars ; the cellar well is said to have its bottom at the level of Náchod square. The castle also includes a French garden , an English park , a game park for deer and mouflons, a military cemetery and bears Ludvík and Dáša , who walk in the castle moat.

Náchod Chateau

This unique military monument from the years 1937 – 1938 was part of extensive border fortifications built to protect Czechoslovakia against Nazi Germany. It was an artillery fort, which was meant to consist of several sub-buildings. In September 1938, construction was completed on the surface only of the Zelený artillery cabin and Můstek and Jeřáb infantry cabins. After the Munich Agreement further work was stopped. Visitors have been exploring the unfinished fortress since 1969. Before descending into the underground, they will see 38 caliber 100 mm mock-howitzers on the top floor, which resemble the main armaments of the cabin. In another part of the exposition, you can see Europe's only collection of miniatures of soldiers of the Czechoslovak Army before World War II. A photo panel documents the events leading up to the Munich Agreement. The area includes a nature trail. The fortress is operated by the Regional Museum in Náchod.

Thousands of rock towers, magical nature, and views that’ll take your breath away. As soon as you step into the Adršpach-Teplice Rocks Nature Reserve, you’ll find yourself in a completely different world. A world, where the tallest tower, The Lovers, reaches 81,4 metres into the sky and water pours over the impressive, 16-metre high Great Waterfall. Discover both, the narrowest alleyway, with a width of only 50 cm, and the picturesque lake with crystal clear water. Come uncover the beauty of nature, as it enchants the visitors in the moment they arrive.

Adršpašské Skály

Adršpach Sandpit is located in the Adršpach-Teplice Rocks National Nature Reserve. The beautiful lake with crystal clear water and white sandy beaches was created by flooding the former sandstone quarry and today is one of the most beautiful rock lakes in the Czech Republic.

Flooded Sand pit in Adrspach

Křížový vrch Nature Reserve (667 m) is located near the village of Adršpach, opposite the Adršpach Rocks. A steep Stations of the Cross leads to the wooded rocky peak with biblical scenes, which are set in the cast iron panels.
